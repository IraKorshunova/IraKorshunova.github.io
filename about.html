I am a master student in Statistical Data Analysis at Ghent University in Belgium. At the same time I'm finishing my master studies in Applied Mathematics at National Technical University of Ukraine, where I received a Bachelor degree in 2012.

My bachelor's thesis was devoted to free-knot splines and their application in functional data analysis. Since finding the optimal knots of the spline is a tricky optimization problem I modified the differential evolution algorithm. Also, I came up with a procedure for choosing the number of knots so that practitioners could choose between computational time and the precision of spline approximation. My approach was successfully applied in IJCNN 2012 competition to classify the saccades measurements. The description of the method, though in Ukrainian, can be found here.

For nearly two years I've been doing a research at Reservoir Lab. Mainly, I've been exploring an application of convolutional neural networks to EEG data for epileptic seizures detection and prediction. I obtained pretty good results, which will be published in a follow-up AES Seizure Prediction Challenge paper. I'm planning to finalize this research by analyzing what convnets can learn from a raw EEG signal (previously I've applied them to sort of spectrograms). Afterwards, I would like to start my PhD in deep learning though I'm still searching for an interesting position.

In my free time I like playing cello and cycling. Currently, I am cellist at Ghent University Symphony Orchestra.
